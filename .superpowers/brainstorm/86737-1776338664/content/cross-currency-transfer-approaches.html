Simple Cross-Currency Transfer: UI Approaches

How should the transfer form look when accounts have different currencies?

A

Inline Dual Amounts

Replace the single Amount row with two rows when a cross-currency transfer is detected. The existing amount field becomes "From Amount" with the source currency, and a new "To Amount" field appears below with the destination currency. Derived exchange rate shown as a subtle label below.

Transfer from USD Account → AUD Account
Payee Currency Exchange
From Amount 100.00 USD
To Amount 155.00 AUD
≈ 1 USD = 1.55 AUD
Date 16 Apr 2026

Pros

  • Minimal UI change — extends existing layout
  • Both amounts visible at once
  • Exchange rate is contextual

Cons

  • "From Amount" / "To Amount" labels only make sense for transfers
B

Amount Stays in Details, Second Amount in Account Section

Keep the primary amount field where it is (in the details section). Add a second amount field in the account section, next to the "To Account" picker. This groups each amount with its respective account.

Pros

  • Each amount sits near its account — clear ownership
  • "Received" label reads naturally

Cons

  • Amounts are visually separated — harder to compare
  • More structural change to the form sections

Recommendation: Option A — keeps the form compact, both amounts are adjacent for easy comparison, and it's a smaller code change (just conditionally showing a second amount row in the existing details section).
